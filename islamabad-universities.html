WELCOME TO ISLAMABAD

About City

Islamabad is the capital city of Pakistan with a population 1.2 million people. This city is build as a planned city in 1960 and the city is renowned for its good living standards, security, tidiness, and plenty of greenery. The master plan for Islamabad was created by the Greek architect Constantinos Apostolou Doxiadis, who divided the city into eight zones: the administrative, diplomatic enclave, residential, educational, industrial, commercial, rural, and green zones. These zones are all managed by the Islamabad Metropolitan Corporation with assistance from the Capital Development Authority.

Education

The majority of Pakistan's educational institutions are located in Islamabad, which also has the highest literacy rate (98%). The presence of several educational institutions in the public and private sectors is significant. Nearly all of the capital's higher education institutions are approved by Pakistan's Higher Education Commission (HEC), whether they are run by privately owned institutions or federally licenced ones. There are total 24 universities in Islamabad.

Tourism

The Faisal Mosque is a significant cultural icon of the city and draws several visitors every day. The Pakistan Monument is a popular tourist destination. This landmark stands for Pakistan's nationalism and sovereignty. Lok Virsa Museum, Institute of Folk and Traditional Heritage Shakarparian Park, and renowned galleries like the National Art Gallery and Gallery 6 are just a few of Islamabad's top attractions.
