WELCOME TO RAWALPINDI

About City

The Pakistani province of Punjab contains the city of Rawalpindi. After Karachi, Lahore, and Faisalabad, it is the third-largest metropolis in Punjab and the fourth-largest city overall in Pakistan. Because of the strong social and economic ties that exist between Rawalpindi and Islamabad, the capital of Pakistan, the two are collectively referred to as the "twin cities".

Education

There are 2,463 government public schools in the Rawalpindi District, of which 1706 are elementary schools, 306 are middle schools, 334 are high schools, and 117 are institutes of higher learning.

After Islamabad and Karachi, Rawalpindi District has the third-highest enrollment rate in Pakistan for students aged 6 to 16 with 97.4%.

Class 5 pupils in Rawalpindi can read sentences in English at a proficiency rate of 77.1%. In Rawalpindi, 27% of kids attend private schools that charge fees.

Recreation

The Arena, a 3D theatre, opened its doors in Rawalpindi's Bahria Town Phase-4 in the middle of 2012.

One of the oldest and largest golf clubs in Pakistan, Rawalpindi Golf Club, built the 27-hole Rawalpindi Golf Course in 1926. An expansive view of Faisal Mosque and the twin towns of Islamabad and Rawalpindi can be seen from the clubhouse. Here, major domestic golf competitions are frequently conducted.
