What makes National Defence University Unique?

Through multidisciplinary research and education programmes, professional exchanges, and outreach with an emphasis on security and modern studies, prepare future leaders from the public and business sectors of Pakistan and friendly nations.

The National Defense University will offer well-considered insights on global defense at the policy and strategic level, advancing the cause of free, open, and rational examination and scholarly discussion in the interest of society.

UNIVERSITY TOUR

In 1962, Commandant Military Staff College Major General (then Commander) Sahabzada Yaqub Khan, who was renowned for his military prowess and intellectual prowess, was requested to develop a war course that would be taught at General and Military College Quetta.

The inaugural Army War Course, which had 12 participants and included one representative from each of the Navy and Air Force, began on May 1st, 1963, under the direction of Military Staff College Quetta. The course's strength was increased to 16 in 1965. The 1965 Pakistan-Indian War prevented there from being a course in 1966. On May 28, 1970, the National Defence College (NDC), housed in the former National Assembly Building, also known as Ayub Hall, was founded in Lalkurti Rawalpindi when it was realised the necessity for a distinct institution. Later, NDC constructed a permanent structure, where the College resided for the following 25 years. The inaugural Commandant of the NDC was Major General Abdul Hamid Khan, SQA, while the Chief Instructor was Brigadier Muhammad Ahmed, SJ (War Wing).
