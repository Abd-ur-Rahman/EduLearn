What makes Bahria University Unique?

Bahria University, a non-profit public higher education school, was established in 2000 and is situated in Islamabad, a major city in Pakistan with a population of between 1 and 5 million people. Additionally, this university has satellite campuses in the following place(s): Karachi. Bahria University (BU), a minor coeducational higher education institution (enrollment range: 5,000â€“5,999 students), is officially accredited/recognized by the Higher Education Commission of Pakistan. Bahria University (BU) offers courses and programmes leading to degrees in a variety of fields of study, including bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees, all of which are recognised by the government. Additionally, BU offers students a number of academic and extracurricular facilities and services, such as a library, housing, financial aid and/or scholarships, as well as administrative support.
